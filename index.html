Interests and Motivations

Working with and for researchers, service providers, journal publishers, library science experts, funders and learned societies in the academic as well as in the commercial and governmental setting, we ensure that data is Findable, Accessible, Interoperable and Reusable. In one word FAIR, for humans as well as for machines.

Tools, Research and Advocacy Areas

We design and develop resources, to improve the collection, curation, representation, sharing and publication of multi-dimensional data. With collaborators worldwide, we lead on and promote: principles for scientific data management, community standards for data sharing, and cultural and policy changes to reward researchers for sharing richly described, high-quality data.
